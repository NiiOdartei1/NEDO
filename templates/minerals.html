Mineral Resource Management

Exploring the discovery, extraction, processing, and sustainable management of minerals that drive our economy and development.

Overview

Mineral Resource Management encompasses the full life‑cycle of mineral assets—from geological exploration and reserve estimation, through environmentally responsible extraction and processing, to reclamation and community engagement. Effective management ensures long‑term economic benefits while safeguarding ecosystems and local livelihoods.

Key Minerals

Gold

Ghana is one of Africa’s top gold producers, with both large‑scale and artisanal operations. Gold mining contributes significantly to export revenues and employment.

Bauxite

Used to produce aluminium, Ghana’s bauxite reserves have high purity levels. Sustainable bauxite mining practices are being adopted to reduce environmental impact.

Manganese

Manganese is vital for steel production. Ghana’s deposits are managed through partnerships to ensure value‑addition locally before export.

Extraction Techniques

  • Open‑pit and underground mining methods
  • Hydraulic mining and dredging for alluvial deposits
  • In‑situ leaching for certain ore bodies
  • Artisanal and small‑scale mining (ASM) practices

Sustainability & Rehabilitation

Modern mineral management emphasizes minimizing ecological footprint, reclaiming mine sites through reforestation and land‑form reshaping, and engaging local communities in benefit‑sharing and oversight.

“Rehabilitation of mined lands not only restores ecosystems but also opens new avenues for agriculture and tourism.”
Ghana Minerals Commission
