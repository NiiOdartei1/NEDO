Water Sustainability

Water sustainability ensures the availability and quality of water for current and future generations, balancing human needs with environmental protection. It supports agriculture, energy, ecosystems, and public health.

Global Water Challenges

Around 2 billion people live in water-stressed countries. Factors such as climate change, overuse, pollution, and poor infrastructure contribute to scarcity. Agricultural irrigation consumes over 70% of freshwater globally.

Strategies for Water Sustainability

  • Efficient Irrigation: Techniques like drip and sprinkler systems minimize waste.
  • Rainwater Harvesting: Collecting and storing rainwater for agriculture and domestic use.
  • Water Recycling: Reusing treated wastewater for irrigation and industrial processes.
  • Watershed Management: Conserving forests and catchment areas to improve water quality and supply.

Facts & Figures

According to the UN, by 2025, half of the world’s population could be living in areas facing water scarcity. Water efficiency technologies in agriculture could reduce global water use by up to 30%.
